# Triathlon Coach Data Model

## 1. Scope and conventions

This is the conceptual data model for the PostgreSQL-backed modular monolith. It defines storage responsibilities and boundaries; it is not an ORM, migration, repository, or API specification.

Delivery labels:

- **MVP:** required by version 1.0. Some MVP entities arrive incrementally in versions 0.2-0.9.
- **Post-MVP:** useful extension after version 1.0, with a boundary reserved now.
- **Future:** AI, coach collaboration, Garmin, and other later roadmap capabilities.

Global rules:

- Every internal primary key is a UUID generated by the application or database. Provider IDs are never primary keys.
- Every athlete-owned row carries `athlete_id` directly or through an unambiguous parent. Direct ownership is preferred on high-volume or security-sensitive tables.
- Store instants as UTC `timestamptz`. Preserve calendar intent with the athlete's IANA `timezone` and, where relevant, the originally scheduled local date/time.
- Store metric units internally: metres, seconds, metres/second, watts, kilograms, Celsius, and beats/minute. Unit preferences affect display only.
- Completed activities and planned workouts are independent. Only a reversible `PlannedCompletedMatch` relates them.
- Common mutable records use `created_at` and `updated_at`; provider records also use `provider_created_at`, `provider_updated_at`, and `last_synced_at` when available.
- `deleted_at` means recoverable soft deletion. Security erasure eventually hard-deletes or irreversibly anonymizes data according to the deletion workflow.

## 2. Access and privacy model

Privacy classes:

| Class | Meaning | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Public** | Intentionally publishable; the MVP publishes nothing by default. | Future public race profile if explicitly enabled |
| **Internal** | Operational data not intended for the athlete's public profile. | Sync state, webhook metadata, algorithm version, audit action |
| **Personal** | Identifies or describes a person or their behavior. | Email, profile, activities, plans, races, equipment |
| **Sensitive** | Health-like, credential, or security data needing tighter policy. | OAuth tokens, pain, illness, wellness, recovery, safety evidence |

The athlete can access their own personal and sensitive data. A coach receives access only through an active, scoped, consented `CoachAthleteRelationship`; health, pain, illness, and recovery scopes are separate from general training access. Operational staff access must be least-privilege, time-limited where possible, and audited. AI providers receive only the minimum redacted evidence required for an accepted feature, never OAuth credentials.

## 3. Entity definitions

### Identity and access

#### User — MVP — Personal

- **Purpose and ID:** authentication/account principal; `id uuid`.
- **Fields:** required `email`, `status`, `auth_subject`, `timezone`, `created_at`, `updated_at`; optional `display_name`, `last_login_at`, `deleted_at`. Email is normalized for comparison; `auth_subject` is the identifier from the selected authentication system.
- **Relationships:** zero or one `AthleteProfile`; zero or one future `CoachProfile`; actor for `AuditEvent` and recipient for `Notification`.
- **Ownership/access:** self-readable/editable within policy; authentication fields are security-restricted. Admin/support access is audited.
- **Constraints/lifecycle:** unique normalized email and unique `auth_subject`; timezone is a valid IANA name. Soft-disable first; verified erasure removes/anonymizes dependent personal data. No external sport-provider ID belongs here.

#### AthleteProfile — MVP — Personal

- **Purpose and ID:** athlete identity and stable training context; `id uuid`.
- **Fields:** required `user_id`, `timezone`, `unit_system` (display preference), `created_at`, `updated_at`; optional `birth_year`, `sex_for_training_context`, `height_m`, `weight_kg`, `experience_level`, `primary_location`, `bio`, `deleted_at`. Sensitive/optional fields require a clear product use.
- **Relationships:** belongs to one `User`; owns settings, zones, races, plans, activities, equipment, wellness, metrics, integrations, and coaching records.
- **Ownership/access:** athlete owns it; active coaches see only granted profile/training scopes. `user_id` unique. Metric values must be positive and plausible without pretending to validate health.
- **Lifecycle:** created during onboarding, updated over time, soft-deleted during account closure then erased under deletion policy.

#### CoachProfile — Future — Personal

- **Purpose and ID:** describes a user acting as a coach; `id uuid`.
- **Fields:** required `user_id`, `display_name`, `created_at`, `updated_at`; optional `qualifications`, `organization`, `bio`, `verification_status`, `deleted_at`.
- **Relationships:** belongs to one `User`; participates in many `CoachAthleteRelationship` records.
- **Ownership/access:** coach edits their profile; athletes see it only in relationship/onboarding contexts. Unique `user_id`; qualifications are claims unless separately verified.
- **Lifecycle:** soft-delete/deactivate while retaining minimal relationship/audit history required for accountability.

#### CoachAthleteRelationship — Future — Sensitive

- **Purpose and ID:** explicit, scoped consent for coach access; `id uuid`.
- **Fields:** required `coach_profile_id`, `athlete_id`, `status`, `scopes`, `requested_at`; optional `accepted_at`, `revoked_at`, `expires_at`, `revoked_by_user_id`, `notes`.
- **Relationships:** joins one coach to one athlete; authorization checks reference it.
- **Ownership/access:** both parties can view; athlete can revoke immediately. Health/recovery access needs an explicit scope and cannot be inferred from workout access.
- **Constraints/lifecycle:** at most one active relationship per coach/athlete pair; accepted/revoked timestamps match state. Retain revoked consent metadata for a defined audit period, then minimize/anonymize.

### Integrations and ingestion

#### IntegrationAccount — MVP (Strava); Future (others) — Personal

- **Purpose and ID:** one athlete's connection to an external provider; `id uuid`.
- **Fields:** required `athlete_id`, `provider`, `external_account_id`, `status`, `scopes`, `created_at`, `updated_at`; optional `display_name`, `provider_metadata`, `connected_at`, `disconnected_at`, `last_synced_at`, `sync_cursor`, `deleted_at`.
- **Relationships:** owns one current `OAuthCredential` where OAuth applies, many `ActivitySource`, `SyncJob`, and `WebhookEvent` records.
- **Ownership/access:** athlete manages status/scopes; credentials and internal cursors are never returned as profile data. Coaches cannot connect/disconnect on the athlete's behalf by default.
- **Constraints/lifecycle:** unique `(athlete_id, provider, external_account_id)` and normally one active connection per athlete/provider. Disconnect revokes/deletes credentials but does not delete canonical activities. Provider ID is text and never a PK.

#### OAuthCredential — MVP — Sensitive

- **Purpose and ID:** encrypted OAuth secret material for an integration account; `id uuid`.
- **Fields:** required `integration_account_id`, encrypted `access_token`, `token_type`, `scopes`, `expires_at`, `key_version`, `created_at`, `updated_at`; optional encrypted `refresh_token`, `provider_token_metadata`, `last_refreshed_at`, `revoked_at`.
- **Relationships:** belongs one-to-one to `IntegrationAccount`.
- **Ownership/access:** service credential subsystem only; athlete may see status/expiry, never token values. No coach, analytics, logs, exports, or AI access.
- **Constraints/lifecycle:** unique active credential per integration account. Ciphertext only in PostgreSQL, encryption keys outside the database. Hard-delete on disconnect/account erasure; rotate/re-encrypt by `key_version`. Never retain raw OAuth responses beyond a short secure diagnostic window.

#### ActivitySource — MVP — Internal

- **Purpose and ID:** provenance/deduplication record connecting a canonical activity to a manual or provider record; `id uuid`.
- **Fields:** required `athlete_id`, `completed_activity_id`, `source_type`, `provider`, `external_activity_id`, `first_seen_at`, `last_seen_at`; optional `integration_account_id`, `external_version`, `source_file_hash`, `provider_created_at`, `provider_updated_at`, `raw_payload_ref`, `raw_payload_expires_at`.
- **Relationships:** belongs to one activity; optionally one integration account. An activity may have multiple sources after explicit duplicate resolution.
- **Ownership/access:** provenance is athlete-visible in summary; raw payload and internal hashes are restricted.
- **Constraints/lifecycle:** provider import unique `(integration_account_id, external_activity_id)`; file import unique `(athlete_id, source_file_hash)` when a stable hash exists. Manual source uses an internal generated source reference. Retain source identity with the activity; delete raw payload after at most 30 days by default unless an active support/replay case justifies a documented extension.

#### SyncJob — MVP — Internal

- **Purpose and ID:** retryable import/synchronization unit and progress record; `id uuid`.
- **Fields:** required `athlete_id`, `integration_account_id`, `job_type`, `status`, `idempotency_key`, `attempt_count`, `created_at`; optional `requested_by_user_id`, `cursor_before`, `cursor_after`, `range_start`, `range_end`, `started_at`, `finished_at`, `next_retry_at`, `error_code`, redacted `error_detail`, `stats`, `parent_job_id`.
- **Relationships:** belongs to integration/athlete; may process many webhook events and create/update many activity sources indirectly.
- **Ownership/access:** athlete sees status and safe errors; workers/support see operational details. Unique `(integration_account_id, job_type, idempotency_key)`.
- **Lifecycle:** terminal jobs retained 90 days for operational history, then summarized or removed; failures must not contain tokens or unnecessary payloads.

#### WebhookEvent — MVP — Internal

- **Purpose and ID:** durable inbox for idempotent provider webhook processing; `id uuid`.
- **Fields:** required `provider`, `deduplication_key`, `event_type`, `received_at`, `status`, `payload_hash`; optional `integration_account_id`, `external_owner_id`, `external_object_id`, `external_event_time`, `processed_at`, `sync_job_id`, redacted `error_detail`, encrypted/restricted `raw_payload`, `raw_payload_expires_at`.
- **Relationships:** optionally resolved to an integration account and processing sync job.
- **Ownership/access:** worker and limited operations access; athlete sees derived sync status, not arbitrary webhook payloads.
- **Constraints/lifecycle:** unique `(provider, deduplication_key)` makes receipt idempotent; processing uses state transitions and safe retries. Keep envelope/status 90 days; purge raw body after 30 days or sooner. Unknown-owner events can be retained briefly for diagnosis, then deleted.

### Athlete configuration

#### AthleteSettings — MVP — Personal

- **Purpose and ID:** athlete preferences separated from identity; `id uuid`.
- **Fields:** required `athlete_id`, `timezone`, `display_unit_system`, `week_starts_on`, `created_at`, `updated_at`; optional `default_pool_length_m`, `language`, `notification_preferences`, `privacy_preferences`.
- **Relationships:** one-to-one with `AthleteProfile`.
- **Ownership/access:** athlete-only edit; coaches may read display/calendar preferences within active training scope. Unique `athlete_id`; valid IANA timezone; pool length positive.
- **Lifecycle:** updated in place with important privacy/consent changes audited; erased with athlete.

#### TrainingZoneSet — MVP — Personal

- **Purpose and ID:** immutable/effective-dated version of zones for a sport and intensity reference; `id uuid`.
- **Fields:** required `athlete_id`, `sport`, `intensity_reference`, `method`, `effective_from`, `created_at`; optional `effective_to`, `threshold_value`, `threshold_unit`, `source`, `confidence`, `notes`, `supersedes_zone_set_id`, `deleted_at`. Threshold references include CSS, FTP, and threshold heart rate.
- **Relationships:** owns ordered `TrainingZone` rows; may supersede another set; metrics/workout interpretations record the set used.
- **Ownership/access:** athlete manages; authorized coach may propose/edit only with granted plan scope. No overlapping active date ranges for the same athlete/sport/reference/method unless explicitly distinguished.
- **Lifecycle:** never mutate historical boundaries after use; close with `effective_to` and create a successor. Soft-delete only unused mistakes; retain used versions with historical records.

#### TrainingZone — MVP — Personal

- **Purpose and ID:** one ordered band within a zone-set version; `id uuid`.
- **Fields:** required `zone_set_id`, `position`, `name`, `lower_bound_type`, `upper_bound_type`; optional numeric `lower_value`, `upper_value`, `unit`, `color`, `description`. Bounds may be absolute or percentages of the parent threshold.
- **Relationships:** belongs to one `TrainingZoneSet`.
- **Ownership/access:** inherited from the zone set. Unique `(zone_set_id, position)` and `(zone_set_id, name)`; bounds must be ordered, non-overlapping, use compatible units, and define inclusive/exclusive semantics.
- **Lifecycle:** immutable with parent after use; retained for historical explainability.

### Races and planning

#### Race — MVP — Personal

- **Purpose and ID:** target event and calendar anchor; `id uuid`.
- **Fields:** required `athlete_id`, `name`, `sport` (often multisport), `start_at`, `timezone`, `priority`, `status`, `created_at`, `updated_at`; optional `distance_m`, structured `disciplines`, `location`, `registration_status`, `course_notes`, `result_duration_s`, `external_url`, `deleted_at`.
- **Relationships:** owns race goals; may be targeted by training plans.
- **Ownership/access:** athlete owns; coach access follows race/plan scope. Start stored UTC plus event timezone; distance metric. No uniqueness by name/date because legitimate duplicates exist.
- **Lifecycle:** planned, completed, cancelled; soft-delete, retaining completed race history unless erased.

#### RaceGoal — MVP — Personal

- **Purpose and ID:** explicit outcome/process goal for a race; `id uuid`.
- **Fields:** required `race_id`, `goal_type`, `priority_order`, `created_at`; optional `target_duration_s`, `target_value`, `unit`, `description`, `achieved`, `evaluated_at`, `updated_at`.
- **Relationships:** belongs to one race.
- **Ownership/access:** inherited from race. Unique `(race_id, priority_order)`; target unit/value must match goal type.
- **Lifecycle:** editable before race, evaluated afterward, retained with race; delete with race/athlete.

#### TrainingPlan — MVP — Personal

- **Purpose and ID:** versioned planning container for a date range and goal; `id uuid`.
- **Fields:** required `athlete_id`, `name`, `start_date`, `end_date`, `timezone`, `status`, `created_at`, `updated_at`; optional `target_race_id`, `objective`, `version`, `created_by_user_id`, `supersedes_plan_id`, `published_at`, `archived_at`, `deleted_at`.
- **Relationships:** optionally targets a race; owns training weeks and workouts; may supersede a plan.
- **Ownership/access:** athlete owns; active coach may create/change under explicit plan scope. Date range valid; only one active published plan should govern a given athlete/date unless overlays are explicitly supported later.
- **Lifecycle:** draft, active, completed, archived. Material published changes are audited; historical completed plans retained.

#### TrainingWeek — MVP — Personal

- **Purpose and ID:** plan grouping and weekly intent, not an analytics result; `id uuid`.
- **Fields:** required `training_plan_id`, `week_number`, `start_date`, `end_date`, `created_at`; optional `phase`, `focus`, `planned_duration_s`, `notes`, `updated_at`.
- **Relationships:** belongs to one plan; contains planned workouts.
- **Ownership/access:** inherited from plan. Unique `(training_plan_id, week_number)` and non-overlapping ordered dates within a plan.
- **Lifecycle:** retained with plan; recalculated summaries do not overwrite declared weekly intent.

#### PlannedWorkout — MVP — Personal

- **Purpose and ID:** scheduled training prescription, separate from execution; `id uuid`.
- **Fields:** required `athlete_id`, `sport`, `title`, `scheduled_start_at`, `timezone`, `status`, `created_at`, `updated_at`; optional `training_plan_id`, `training_week_id`, `workout_type`, `description`, `planned_duration_s`, `planned_distance_m`, `intensity_reference`, `target_zone_set_id`, `target_rpe`, `location_type`, `equipment_id`, `source`, `version`, `cancelled_at`, `deleted_at`.
- **Relationships:** belongs optionally to plan/week; owns ordered steps; may reference zones/equipment; has zero or more match history rows but at most one active completed match.
- **Ownership/access:** athlete owns; authorized coach can plan. Metric units only; sport is swimming, cycling, running, strength, multisport, or other. Schedule is UTC with timezone/local intent preserved.
- **Lifecycle:** draft, scheduled, completed-by-match, skipped, cancelled. Rescheduling changes this record/audit, never the completed activity.

#### PlannedWorkoutStep — MVP — Personal

- **Purpose and ID:** ordered sport-specific interval, set, or strength prescription; `id uuid`.
- **Fields:** required `planned_workout_id`, `position`, `step_type`; optional `parent_step_id`, `repeat_count`, `duration_s`, `distance_m`, `target_type`, `target_min`, `target_max`, `target_unit`, `zone_id`, `stroke`, `cadence_rpm`, `repetitions`, `sets`, `load_kg`, `rest_s`, `exercise_name`, `notes`.
- **Relationships:** belongs to a workout; optional parent supports repeat groups; may reference one zone.
- **Ownership/access:** inherited from workout. Unique `(planned_workout_id, position)` within a stable ordering scheme; fields must be valid for sport/step type and target reference.
- **Lifecycle:** version with the workout; retain the prescription used for comparison rather than rewriting history.

### Completed activities

#### CompletedActivity — MVP — Personal

- **Purpose and ID:** canonical completed session independent of source or plan; `id uuid`.
- **Fields:** required `athlete_id`, `sport`, `name`, `start_at`, `timezone`, `elapsed_time_s`, `source_summary`, `created_at`, `updated_at`; optional `moving_time_s`, `distance_m`, `elevation_gain_m`, `average/max_heart_rate_bpm`, `average/max_power_w`, `average_speed_mps`, `calories_kcal`, `rpe`, `session_rpe_load`, `indoor`, `commute`, `description`, `provider_deleted_at`, `deleted_at`.
- **Relationships:** owns laps/streams/sources/equipment usage; matched through `PlannedCompletedMatch`; contributes to deterministic metrics/evidence.
- **Ownership/access:** athlete owns; coach activity access requires explicit scope. Sensitive subjective fields may need narrower recovery scope. Times/distance non-negative; RPE on documented 1-10 scale.
- **Lifecycle:** imported/manual, corrected, provider-removed, soft-deleted. Provider updates change only provider-owned mapped fields under a documented merge policy; athlete edits are not silently overwritten.

#### ActivityLap — MVP when supplied — Personal

- **Purpose and ID:** normalized summary for a provider/manual lap or segment; `id uuid`.
- **Fields:** required `completed_activity_id`, `lap_index`, `start_at`, `elapsed_time_s`; optional `external_lap_id`, `moving_time_s`, `distance_m`, `average/max_heart_rate_bpm`, `average_power_w`, `average_speed_mps`, `cadence`, `elevation_gain_m`, `provider_metadata`.
- **Relationships:** belongs to one activity.
- **Ownership/access:** inherited from activity. Unique `(completed_activity_id, lap_index)`; optional external ID unique within its activity/source. Ordered and time-bounded by activity where provider data permits.
- **Lifecycle:** replace/upsert as one provider-owned set using source version; delete with activity. Do not retain redundant raw lap payload after mapping/retention window.

#### ActivityStream — Post-MVP — Personal (Sensitive for location)

- **Purpose and ID:** reference or chunk of dense time-series samples; `id uuid`.
- **Fields:** required `completed_activity_id`, `stream_type`, `storage_kind`, `sample_count`, `resolution`, `created_at`; optional `start_at`, `sample_interval_ms`, `unit`, `object_key`, compressed `samples`, `checksum`, `source_id`, `retention_until`. Stream types include time, distance, heart rate, power, pace/speed, cadence, altitude, temperature, and location.
- **Relationships:** belongs to activity and optionally source.
- **Ownership/access:** activity rules apply; location and physiological streams require stricter download/access logging and minimization.
- **Constraints/lifecycle:** unique `(completed_activity_id, stream_type, source_id, resolution)`; checksum validates immutable content. MVP stores summaries, not dense streams. Decide partitioned PostgreSQL versus encrypted object storage after measuring volume. Delete with activity; raw/high-resolution location may use shorter configurable retention.

#### PlannedCompletedMatch — MVP — Personal

- **Purpose and ID:** explicit, explainable, reversible association between plan and execution; `id uuid`.
- **Fields:** required `athlete_id`, `planned_workout_id`, `completed_activity_id`, `match_method`, `status`, `created_at`; optional `confidence`, `matched_by_user_id`, `reason`, `confirmed_at`, `reversed_at`, `reversed_by_user_id`, `reversal_reason`.
- **Relationships:** joins one planned workout and one completed activity.
- **Ownership/access:** athlete can create, confirm, or reverse; coach may suggest/confirm only with scope, with actor recorded.
- **Constraints/lifecycle:** partial unique indexes enforce at most one active match per planned workout and per completed activity; suggested automatic matches are not equivalent to confirmed matches. Reversal closes the row rather than deleting it, preserving auditability.

### Equipment

#### Equipment — MVP — Personal

- **Purpose and ID:** athlete-owned gear and maintenance context; `id uuid`.
- **Fields:** required `athlete_id`, `equipment_type`, `name`, `status`, `created_at`, `updated_at`; optional `brand`, `model`, `nickname`, `purchase_date`, `initial_distance_m`, `service_interval_distance_m`, `service_interval_duration_s`, `retired_at`, `notes`, `external_provider`, `external_equipment_id`, `deleted_at`.
- **Relationships:** referenced by planned workouts and equipment usage.
- **Ownership/access:** athlete owns; coach may read/assign with equipment scope. Provider gear identity unique `(athlete_id, external_provider, external_equipment_id)` when present; name need not be unique.
- **Lifecycle:** active, maintenance, retired; soft-delete only when unused, otherwise retire and retain history.

#### EquipmentUsage — MVP — Personal

- **Purpose and ID:** auditable allocation of completed activity use to equipment; `id uuid`.
- **Fields:** required `athlete_id`, `equipment_id`, `completed_activity_id`, `usage_source`, `created_at`; optional `distance_m`, `duration_s`, `assigned_by_user_id`, `removed_at`.
- **Relationships:** joins equipment and activity.
- **Ownership/access:** inherited from both; athlete controls assignments. Unique active `(equipment_id, completed_activity_id)`; allocation cannot exceed relevant activity totals without an explicit multisport/component rule.
- **Lifecycle:** reversible by `removed_at`; usage totals are derived, never authoritative counters on equipment.

### Wellness, pain, and deterministic metrics

#### WellnessEntry — MVP — Sensitive

- **Purpose and ID:** athlete-reported daily recovery context; `id uuid`.
- **Fields:** required `athlete_id`, `local_date`, `timezone`, `created_at`, `updated_at`; optional `sleep_duration_s`, `sleep_quality`, `fatigue`, `soreness`, `stress`, `mood`, `illness_status`, `resting_heart_rate_bpm`, `hrv_ms`, `notes`, `source`, `deleted_at`.
- **Relationships:** may contribute to recovery metrics and coaching evidence; pain detail lives in `InjuryOrPainEntry`.
- **Ownership/access:** athlete and explicitly health-scoped coach only; support access exceptional and audited. Unique `(athlete_id, local_date, source)`; scales and collection time are documented.
- **Lifecycle:** corrections retained in audit history where necessary; soft-delete then erase. Device-derived and self-reported values retain source and must not be silently conflated.

#### RecoveryMetric — MVP — Sensitive

- **Purpose and ID:** deterministic, versioned recovery/readiness output, not a diagnosis; `id uuid`.
- **Fields:** required `athlete_id`, `local_date`, `metric_type`, `value`, `unit`, `algorithm_version`, `calculated_at`, `data_window_start`, `data_window_end`; optional `coverage`, `confidence`, `explanation`, `input_fingerprint`, `superseded_at`.
- **Relationships:** derived from wellness, activity, pain/illness, and training metrics; may be coaching evidence.
- **Ownership/access:** athlete and health-scoped coach; AI only through minimized evidence. Unique active `(athlete_id, local_date, metric_type, algorithm_version)`.
- **Lifecycle:** append/recompute and supersede, never overwrite a historical algorithm result. Delete/recompute when source evidence is erased.

#### InjuryOrPainEntry — MVP — Sensitive

- **Purpose and ID:** voluntary record of pain, injury concern, or symptom context used for safety gates; `id uuid`.
- **Fields:** required `athlete_id`, `entry_type`, `onset_at`, `status`, `created_at`, `updated_at`; optional `body_location`, `severity`, `description`, `related_activity_id`, `resolved_at`, `medical_attention_sought`, `training_impact`, `deleted_at`.
- **Relationships:** optionally references a completed activity; contributes to recovery/safety evidence.
- **Ownership/access:** athlete-only by default; separately consented health-scoped coach access. Never expose to ordinary integration providers or include free text in AI context without minimization.
- **Constraints/lifecycle:** severity uses a documented scale; resolved date follows onset. Soft-delete then privacy erasure; safety decisions should preserve only minimal non-identifying audit facts if legally/operationally required.

#### WeeklyTrainingMetric — MVP — Personal; Sensitive when recovery-related

- **Purpose and ID:** deterministic weekly aggregate by athlete and optionally sport; `id uuid`.
- **Fields:** required `athlete_id`, `week_start_date`, `timezone`, `sport_scope`, `metric_type`, `value`, `unit`, `algorithm_version`, `calculated_at`; optional `coverage`, `input_fingerprint`, `zone_set_id`, `explanation`, `superseded_at`.
- **Relationships:** derived from activities, plans, matches, zones, and wellness as appropriate; usable as coaching evidence.
- **Ownership/access:** athlete; coach scope depends on metric inputs. Unique active `(athlete_id, week_start_date, sport_scope, metric_type, algorithm_version)`.
- **Lifecycle:** immutable calculation runs are superseded on recomputation. Deterministic identity/version must distinguish them from AI interpretation.

#### DailyTrainingMetric — MVP — Personal; Sensitive when recovery-related

- **Purpose and ID:** deterministic daily aggregate/trend input; `id uuid`.
- **Fields:** required `athlete_id`, `local_date`, `timezone`, `sport_scope`, `metric_type`, `value`, `unit`, `algorithm_version`, `calculated_at`; optional `coverage`, `input_fingerprint`, `zone_set_id`, `explanation`, `superseded_at`.
- **Relationships:** same evidence sources as weekly metrics; weekly metrics may aggregate daily results but retain their own version/fingerprint.
- **Ownership/access:** same scoped rules as weekly metrics. Unique active `(athlete_id, local_date, sport_scope, metric_type, algorithm_version)`.
- **Lifecycle:** append/supersede; erase/recompute when inputs are deleted. TSS/CTL/ATL/TSB, if added, are explicitly typed/versioned metrics with documented limitations, never hidden profile fields.

### Coaching, communication, and audit

#### CoachingRecommendation — Future — Personal; Sensitive if health evidence used

- **Purpose and ID:** explainable AI or rule-generated interpretation/proposal; `id uuid`.
- **Fields:** required `athlete_id`, `recommendation_type`, `generator_type`, `status`, `summary`, `created_at`; optional `detail`, `proposed_change`, `model_provider`, `model_name`, `prompt_version`, `algorithm_version`, `confidence`, `uncertainty`, `expires_at`, `decided_at`, `decided_by_user_id`, `decision_reason`, `applied_at`, `superseded_at`.
- **Relationships:** has one or more evidence links; may propose, but never directly own, plan changes.
- **Ownership/access:** athlete controls decision; active authorized coach may review. Health-derived recommendations inherit sensitive classification. `generator_type` distinguishes deterministic rule output from AI interpretation.
- **Constraints/lifecycle:** AI recommendations require stored evidence, model/prompt provenance, uncertainty, and explicit acceptance before material mutation. Retain decision/audit history; delete narrative/evidence with athlete subject to minimal security audit retention.

#### CoachingEvidence — Future — classification inherited from evidence

- **Purpose and ID:** immutable citation from a recommendation to stored evidence; `id uuid`.
- **Fields:** required `recommendation_id`, `evidence_type`, `evidence_entity_id`, `relationship`, `captured_at`; optional `evidence_version`, `excerpt_or_fact`, `freshness_at_use`, `quality_flag`, `input_hash`.
- **Relationships:** belongs to recommendation; polymorphically references an allowed stored entity such as workout, activity, metric, wellness entry, race, zone set, or pain entry.
- **Ownership/access:** cannot broaden access beyond either recommendation or source evidence; sensitive evidence stays sensitive. Unique `(recommendation_id, evidence_type, evidence_entity_id, relationship)`.
- **Lifecycle:** retain with recommendation for explainability; if source is erased, remove sensitive snapshot and mark evidence unavailable rather than preserving prohibited data. Application validation must enforce allowed type/ID pairs because a generic FK cannot.

#### Notification — Post-MVP — Personal

- **Purpose and ID:** in-app/email delivery intent for reminders, sync failures, equipment alerts, or coaching updates; `id uuid`.
- **Fields:** required `user_id`, `notification_type`, `channel`, `status`, `created_at`; optional `athlete_id`, `title`, `body`, structured `data`, `scheduled_at`, `sent_at`, `read_at`, `failed_at`, `deduplication_key`, `expires_at`.
- **Relationships:** belongs to recipient user and optional athlete context.
- **Ownership/access:** recipient only; content must avoid sensitive health detail in insecure channels. Unique `(user_id, channel, deduplication_key)` when supplied.
- **Lifecycle:** delivery logs retained 90 days; in-app items retained up to one year or until deletion. Do not store provider credentials or full evidence in notification payloads.

#### AuditEvent — MVP — Internal; may be Sensitive

- **Purpose and ID:** append-only accountability for security, consent, privacy, coach access, matching, recommendation decisions, and material plan changes; `id uuid`.
- **Fields:** required `action`, `occurred_at`, `outcome`, `request_id`; optional `actor_user_id`, `athlete_id`, `entity_type`, `entity_id`, `ip_hash`, `user_agent_summary`, `reason`, redacted `metadata`.
- **Relationships:** references actors/athletes/entities logically without controlling their lifecycle.
- **Ownership/access:** restricted security/operations; athlete receives relevant consent/access history through a safe projection. Never store tokens, raw health notes, or full before/after payloads.
- **Constraints/lifecycle:** append-only; index request/action/actor/entity/time. Retain security events for a documented period (recommended 12 months), then delete or anonymize; retain deletion proof without retaining deleted personal content.

## 4. Sports and intensity vocabulary

All sport-bearing entities use: `swimming`, `cycling`, `running`, `strength`, `multisport`, `other`. Multisport records should use structured discipline order/details where needed; do not invent separate internal IDs for sport names in the MVP.

Supported intensity references are `heart_rate`, `power`, `pace`, `speed`, `rpe`, `session_rpe`, `swimming_pace`, `css`, `ftp`, and `threshold_heart_rate`. CSS, FTP, and threshold heart rate can be both threshold methods/values and references for zone derivation. Units must be explicit and compatible: bpm, watts, seconds/metre (or a normalized pace representation), metres/second, or dimensionless documented scales.

## 5. Recommended PostgreSQL types

| Concept | PostgreSQL type | Guidance |
|---|---|---|
| Internal ID/FK | `uuid` | Never provider text/numeric IDs |
| Instant | `timestamptz` | UTC connection/session policy |
| Athlete/event local date | `date` | Pair with IANA timezone where calendar meaning matters |
| IANA timezone | `text` | Validate in application; do not store numeric UTC offset alone |
| Duration | `integer` or `bigint` seconds | Milliseconds only where streams require them |
| Distance/elevation | `double precision` metres | Check non-negative where appropriate |
| Speed | `double precision` metres/second | Derive display pace/speed |
| Power/HR/cadence | `double precision` or `integer` | Preserve required precision; bpm normally integer |
| Weight/load | `numeric(10,3)` kilograms | Avoid binary drift for entered strength loads |
| Scale/score | `smallint` or `numeric` | Constrain documented range |
| Provider ID/cursor | `text` | Providers may exceed integer ranges/change formats |
| Status/category | PostgreSQL enum or constrained `text` | Prefer constrained text if enum evolution is frequent |
| Scopes/tags | `text[]` | Small bounded sets; normalize if they gain behavior |
| Structured metadata | `jsonb` | Provider/algorithm detail only, not core searchable fields |
| Raw payload | encrypted `bytea` or object reference | Short retention, access-restricted |
| Hash/fingerprint | `text` or `bytea` | Include algorithm/version convention |

Avoid `float` for timestamps, local timestamps without timezone, imperial persisted values, arbitrary JSON for core workout/activity attributes, and database arrays for large streams.

## 7. Indexing strategy

- Every PK has its UUID index; every FK used for ownership or joins gets an index, led by `athlete_id` where athlete-scoped queries dominate.
- Activities: `(athlete_id, start_at desc)`, `(athlete_id, sport, start_at desc)`, and partial index on non-deleted rows.
- Planned workouts: `(athlete_id, scheduled_start_at)`, `(training_plan_id, scheduled_start_at)`, and active status/date partial indexes.
- Calendar/races: `(athlete_id, start_at)`; metrics: `(athlete_id, local_date/week_start_date, metric_type, sport_scope)`.
- Zones: `(athlete_id, sport, intensity_reference, effective_from desc)` with an exclusion constraint or transaction validation preventing unintended effective-range overlap.
- Integration dedupe: unique `(integration_account_id, external_activity_id)` and `(provider, deduplication_key)`; sync polling indexes on status/next retry.
- Matching/equipment usage: partial unique indexes for active associations (`reversed_at is null`, `removed_at is null`).
- Wellness/pain: `(athlete_id, local_date desc)` and `(athlete_id, status, onset_at desc)` under stricter database roles/policies.
- Audit: BRIN on `occurred_at` when volume warrants, plus B-tree `(actor_user_id, occurred_at desc)` and `(entity_type, entity_id, occurred_at desc)`.
- Use GIN on `jsonb` only for demonstrated query paths. Avoid indexing encrypted values, raw payloads, long notes, and every possible metric column prematurely.
- Consider time/range partitioning only after measured volume, especially for webhook/audit records and activity streams.

## 8. Provider synchronization and deduplication

1. Resolve a webhook or poll to an `IntegrationAccount`; store receipt using a provider-derived stable deduplication key. If no event ID exists, use a versioned hash of stable envelope fields.
2. A unique webhook constraint makes repeated delivery return the existing receipt. Workers claim state atomically and retries reuse the same event/job identity.
3. Fetch authoritative provider data when possible; normalize it into `CompletedActivity`, `ActivityLap`, and an `ActivitySource` within one transaction/outbox boundary.
4. Deduplicate first by `(integration_account_id, external_activity_id)`. Never merge across providers using time/distance heuristics without an explicit athlete-confirmed duplicate workflow.
5. Track provider version/update time and ignore stale out-of-order updates. A provider delete marks provenance/provider deletion; it does not automatically erase athlete-owned edits or other sources.
6. Keep manual fields and provider-owned fields under an explicit merge policy. Provider refreshes cannot silently overwrite athlete RPE, notes, matches, or manual corrections.
7. Persist cursors only after durable processing. Jobs are safe to replay; metric/equipment downstream tasks use their own idempotency keys.
8. Raw payloads are exceptional: restricted/encrypted, referenced from source/event, default maximum retention 30 days, never a substitute for normalized records.

## 9. Data deletion and export

An export includes identity/profile, settings, zones with history, races/goals, plans/workouts/steps, completed activities/laps and available streams, provenance summaries, reversible matches, equipment/usage, wellness/pain/recovery, deterministic metrics with versions, coaching recommendations/evidence, notification history where useful, and a safe integration/sync history. Use documented machine-readable formats and UTC timestamps plus recorded timezones. Never export OAuth token values, internal encryption data, or other users' data.

Deletion is an asynchronous, auditable workflow:

1. Disable access, revoke providers, hard-delete OAuth credentials, and stop/neutralize queued jobs.
2. Delete athlete-owned sensitive data and raw payloads first, then personal plans, activities, metrics, equipment, integrations, and profiles in dependency-safe order.
3. Delete or anonymize coach relationship counterparts and notifications without deleting the coach's own account.
4. Remove external object-store streams and caches; mark backup retention accurately. Recommended encrypted backup expiry is no more than 30 days for the MVP.
5. Retain only minimal non-content security/deletion proof where justified, with actor/entity identifiers anonymized or pseudonymized.
6. Report pending, completed, or failed state honestly. Retrying deletion must be idempotent.

Disconnecting a provider is not account deletion: revoke credentials and stop synchronization while preserving canonical athlete activities until the athlete separately deletes them.

## 10. Explicit decisions

1. UUIDs are internal IDs; provider identifiers are scoped text alternate keys.
2. One `User` may gain athlete and/or coach roles; profiles are separate to avoid role columns and permit future dual-role users.
3. Athlete ownership is explicit throughout the model even though the initial product serves one athlete.
4. Plans, workouts, completed activities, and their match are separate lifecycles. Matches are reversible history, not nullable FKs on either side.
5. Zone sets are effective-dated immutable versions; calculations cite the version used.
6. Canonical activities are provider-neutral. `ActivitySource` contains provenance and dedupe identity.
7. Summary activities/laps are MVP; dense streams are post-MVP and storage technology remains intentionally undecided.
8. Metric rows are typed, versioned deterministic outputs. AI prose never occupies deterministic metric rows.
9. AI recommendations require evidence links and explicit acceptance for material plan changes.
10. Wellness, pain, illness, recovery, physiological streams, and credentials receive stricter access than ordinary training data.
11. Metric units are canonical internally; original/display units may be retained only as provenance/presentation metadata.
12. JSONB is reserved for bounded provider/algorithm metadata, not used to avoid modeling core fields.

6. Are activity laps necessary in version 0.4 or can they arrive with comparison/analysis while preserving provider compatibility?
7. After measuring volume, should dense streams use partitioned PostgreSQL, encrypted object storage, or a hybrid, and what location retention should apply?
